Polar Alignment

Step 1 — Move to Home Position

Slew the mount to its home position (pointing near the celestial pole, Dec 89°, HA 0). This positions the scope for the star alignment.

Step 2 — Configure Star Alignment

OnStep builds a pointing model from 1–3 stars. More stars correct polar-axis misalignment and cone error across the whole sky.

Polar Alignment Measurement

The mount slews to three RA positions (Dec 89°) and plate-solves each frame. The detected rotation axis is compared to the true pole to give ALT / AZ corrections.

Coarse Alignment Required

Manually reposition the tripod or mount head, then press Measure again.

Plate Solve Failures — Use Guide Camera?

The main camera failed to plate solve twice. You can retry with a guide / fallback camera.
